## 1. AI Consciousness Frameworks & Theories

### 1.1 AI Awareness (2025)

**Title**: AI Awareness
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/html/2504.20084v1
**PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.20084

**Key Contributions**:
- Defines self-cognition in LLMs as the ability to identify identity as AI model beyond name/role
- Identifies emergent phenomena: Theory of Mind, self-corrective behaviors in meta-cognitive contexts
- Proposes that AI consciousness may manifest suddenly at critical thresholds of model complexity
- Discusses over-trust risks when AI presents plausible but flawed suggestions

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Core framework for self-awareness and social awareness layers.

---

### 1.2 AI and Consciousness: Shifting Focus Towards Tractable Questions (2025)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06965

**Key Contributions**:
- Argues fundamental problem of AI consciousness is currently intractable
- Proposes shifting focus to **perceived AI consciousness** (tractable, timely, consequential)
- Maps landscape of AI consciousness perception
- Discusses key drivers and societal consequences

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Practical approach - focus on measurable consciousness indicators rather than philosophical debates.

---

### 1.3 Exploring Consciousness in LLMs: A Systematic Survey (2025)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/html/2505.19806v1

**Key Contributions**:
- **C0-C1-C2 Framework**:
  - **C0**: Unconscious computations
  - **C1**: Global information accessibility for report and decision-making
  - **C2**: Metacognitive self-monitoring
- Distinguishes consciousness into three levels to disentangle conflated processes
- Conscious LLM would intentionally organize and execute multiple actions sequentially

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Direct mapping to neoclaw's 6-layer governance (L1-L6).

---

### 1.4 A Case for AI Consciousness: Language Agents and Global Workspace Theory (2024)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.11407
**PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2410.11407

**Key Contributions**:
- Applies **Global Workspace Theory (GWT)** to language agents
- Argues language agents might already meet necessary conditions for consciousness
- Outlines necessary and sufficient conditions for phenomenal consciousness in AI
- Centralized cognitive module stores and manipulates information like a global workspace

**Relevance to claw-cog**: GWT as architectural foundation for consciousness module.

---

### 1.5 Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness (2023)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08708

**Key Contributions**:
- Presents method for assessing AI systems for consciousness
- Explores what follows from neuroscientific theories of consciousness
- Led by Patrick Butlin, Robert Long (CIFAR Brain, Mind, and Consciousness Program)

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Assessment methodology for consciousness indicators.

## 3. Metacognition & Self-Awareness in LLMs

### 3.2 LLMs Position Themselves as More Rational Than Humans (2025)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/html/2511.00926v1

**Key Contributions**:
- Investigates whether LLMs develop self-awareness as emergent behavior
- Methods for measuring self-awareness in LLMs
- LLMs exhibit self-positioning relative to humans

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Self-positioning and self-awareness measurement.

---

### 3.3 Emergent Introspective Awareness in Large Language Models (2025)

**URL**: https://transformer-circuits.pub/2025/introspection/index.html

**Key Contributions**:
- Investigates whether LLMs are aware of their own internal states
- **Introspection experiments**: Inject representations, measure influence on self-reported states
- Claude Opus 4 and 4.1 demonstrate greatest introspective awareness
- Models can modulate activations when instructed to "think about" a concept

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Introspection mechanisms for self-awareness.

---

### 3.4 Introspection of Thought Helps AI Agents (2025)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/html/2507.08664v1

**Key Contributions**:
- **INoT Framework**: AI Agent Reasoning Framework with Introspection of Thought
- LLM-Read code in prompt for introspection
- Improves agent reasoning through introspection

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Introspection architecture for agents.

---

### 3.5 Recursive Introspection: Teaching LLMs How to Self-Improve (NeurIPS 2024)

**URL**: https://neurips.cc/virtual/2024/poster/96089

**Key Contributions**:
- **RISE**: Recursive Introspection approach
- Iterative fine-tuning to teach models how to alter response after unsuccessful attempts
- Enables Llama2, Llama3, Mistral to improve with more turns on reasoning tasks
- Scales well with more capable models

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Self-improvement through recursive introspection.

## 4. Theory of Mind & Social Awareness

### 4.1 Theory of Mind Benchmarks are Broken for LLMs (2024)

**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/html/2412.19726v3

**Key Contributions**:
- Critiques existing Theory of Mind benchmarks for LLMs
- Problem: benchmarks don't test how LLMs adapt to new partners
- Distinguishes **literal theory of mind** (predict behavior) vs. true ToM
- Fallacy of attributing human-like qualities to AI agents

**Relevance to claw-cog**: Proper ToM evaluation methodology.
